FAQ

What is Majority Judgment?
Majority Judgment is a recently proposed voting procedure that requires voters to provide grades to each candidate. Each candidates' Majority Grade is determined, which is then used to find the Majority Ranking among them. Unlike traditional voting procedures that just tell you the winner, and the percentage of voters who vote for each candidate, Majority Judgment produces a much finer-grained summary of the poll. Each voter gets to express her/his preferences in a much more intuitive way than ranking or selecting their best.
What is involved in setting up a Majority Judgment poll?
Two pieces of information.
  1. List of candidates.
    2. e.g., emacs, vi, notepad++
  2. Ordered list of allowed grades.
    3. e.g., hate it, never heard of it / don't care, like it, absolutely love it.
